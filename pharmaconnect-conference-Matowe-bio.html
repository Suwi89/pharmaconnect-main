Matowe



Dr. Lloyd Matowe, PhD, MSc, BPharm (Hons) Dr Matowe is a Director with the organization, Pharmaceutical Systems Africa (PSA). www.pharmasystafrica.com and PICMA Africa Limited https://www.picma-scm.com/. Both organizations work with international organizations, national governments, and implementing partners to strengthen pharmacy and supply chain systems across Africa. PICMA and PSA have offices PSA has offices in Ghana, Liberia, Malawi, Nigeria, South Africa, Tanzania, Uganda, Zambia & Zimbabwe.

Dr Matowe also serves as the Dean & Director of the School of Pharmacy at Manica University, in Lusaka, Zambia. In this capacity, he works with the pharmacy fraternity to make the training of pharmacists in Zambia professional, enriching, and enjoyable. At Manica, he has created a number of students scholarships both at undergraduate and at post graduate level. Across Africa, from Angola to Zimbabwe, over 200 students have benefited from his generous scholarships. He enjoys working with students and creating a rewarding pharmacy future. He is currently the patron of the Zambia Pharmacy Students Association and annually he sponsors a professional and scientific conference that brings pharmacy students from across Zambia, together.

Outside the classroom, Dr Matowe serves on a number of international committees including chairing the People that Deliver Initiative (PtD), serving on the Global Fund Market Dynamics Committee, the Global Health Supply Chain summit, among others. He is a co-founder and co-director of PICMA Africa Limited: https://www.picma-scm.com/our-team. PICMA is cross Africa multiple million-dollar consultancy firm. Dr Matowe has worked in over 20 countries in Africa including working with multilateral donor agencies and implementing partners. In addition, he has worked in the UK, the USA, Europe and the Middle East, all in the field of his loved profession, pharmacy.

In academia, Dr Matowe has taught at various universities including serving as a visiting lecturer at MUHAS in Tanzania, KNUST in Ghana, University of Benin in Nigeria, University of Liberia, Makerere University in Uganda, TUT in South Africa, the University of Iowa in the USA, among many others. Dr Matowe holds a PhD in Public Health Systems & Policy, an MSc in Clinical Pharmacology & Therapeutics (both from University of Aberdeen in the UK,) a diploma in M&E from Johns Hopkins University in the USA, and a Bachelor of Pharmacy degree (Hons) from the University of Zimbabwe. He is a proud pharmacist and is never shy of showing it. Such a noble, unparalleled profession.
